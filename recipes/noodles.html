Chef John's Drunken Noodles


Description

The perfect recipe for alcoholics! According to its inventor, this noodle dish is a spicy, sweet, and savory bowl of noodles invented by and for drunk people. However, there is another theory floating about claiming that the noodle dish's name derives from the fact that the recipe once used wine in its sauce. Regardless of how drunken noodles came about, it is an easy recipe that yields incredibly delicious noodles!

The key to perfecting this dish lies in preparing the rice noodles. No matter what size or thickness you're using, you need to soak them in hot water until they are very flexible but not soft. Avoid boiling the water despite the "Steps" portion of the recipe below, as you may overcook the noodles accidentally and become overly starchy; After all, rice noodles are fast to cook. While it is recommended to use rice noodles, you can use any noodle or pasta with this sauce with incredible results!

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces dried rice noodles
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon asian fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup shallots thinly sliced
  • 6 teaspoons chile padi thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • 2 1/2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs cut into strips (Optional)
  • 1 pound Chinese broccoli sliced (Optional)
  • 4 green onions thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh thai basil leaves

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in hot water until flexible, 10 to 15 minutes. Occasionally move the noodles around to separate them. Drain the water and cover with a wet paper towel.
  2. Combine oyster sauce, soy sauce, Asian fish sauce, maple syrup, sugar, and water in a small bowl.
  3. Heat vegetable oil and sesame oil in a wok, or large frying pan, over medium-high heat. Stir-fry shallots, chile peppers, and garlic until shallots are soft, about 2 minutes. Move the mixture around the outside of the pan to create a space and add chicken. Sear for 1 minute, then combine with shallot mixture. Add Chinese broccoli stems and stir-fry until tender, about 2 minutes. Add Chinese broccoli leaves and stir-fry until wilted.
  4. Add drunken noodle sauce and sliced green onions and mix. Cook until sauce begins to simmer. Add soaked rice noodles and toss to coat until noodles have absorbed the sauce and are cooked through, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat.
  5. Stir in Thai basil leaves until wilted. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve.
